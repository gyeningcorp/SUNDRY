# Go-Gig Youth Provider Program

**Status:** Concept draft — 2026-05-25  
**Goal:** Let 16+ youth participate in Go-Gig in a tightly controlled way, with parent/guardian consent, parent-controlled payouts, and strong platform-positioning language.  
**Important:** Go-Gig can reduce risk, but it cannot guarantee "no liability." This program should be reviewed by counsel before launch.

---

## Strategic Position

Best near-term version:
- Focus first on **ages 16-17**, not younger minors
- Position Go-Gig as a **technology marketplace only**, not an employer, staffing agency, trainer, or supervisor
- Make the **parent/guardian the legal account owner and payout recipient** for under-18 providers
- Restrict youth providers to **low-risk, non-hazardous, local service categories**
- Require explicit parental consent, category restrictions, and safety acknowledgments

Why 16+ first:
- cleaner trust story than opening with 13-15
- simpler operations and public perception
- still captures the “high school talent” growth angle
- easier to build a pilot around school, church, and local community referrals

---

## Reality Check on Liability

You can lower exposure substantially, but you cannot honestly promise **zero liability**.

Better internal/legal framing:
- Minimize platform liability
- Avoid employer classification
- Push operational responsibility to parent/guardian and provider
- Avoid direct supervision/control that makes Go-Gig look like the employer
- Avoid unsafe categories and risky workflows
- Create strong records of consent, disclosures, and policy acceptance

Key point:
**If Go-Gig curates minors into unsafe work, handles them like employees, or ignores complaints/incidents, disclaimers alone will not save the platform.**

## Recommended Program Structure

### 1. Account Structure
For any provider under 18:
- Parent/guardian creates or controls the main account
- Youth profile exists as a sub-profile under that adult account
- Parent/guardian is the contracting party for payouts and platform terms
- Parent/guardian receives all legal notices, incident notices, and payout records

### 2. Category Restrictions
Allow only low-risk categories at launch:
- tutoring
- homework help
- lawn care, basic only
- car washing by hand
- pet sitting / dog walking
- photography
- graphic design
- video editing
- tech help
- party setup / teardown, light duty only

Block at launch:
- roofing
- ladders above a defined limit
- childcare unless separately reviewed
- driving-based work
- food delivery using vehicles
- heavy lifting
- construction
- electrical/plumbing/HVAC
- hazardous tools or chemicals
- overnight work
- any job in private settings with unclear supervision

### 3. Payout Structure
For under-18 providers:
- payouts go to the **parent/guardian account only**
- Go-Gig does not pay minors directly at launch
- parent/guardian handles transfer of earnings to the youth off-platform
- platform records should clearly show that funds were paid to the adult account holder

This is the safest first version operationally.

### 4. Messaging & Safety
- in-app messaging only
- phone/email hidden until booking stage, or not shown at all for youth accounts
- parent copied on all key messages or approvals
- customers must acknowledge they are booking a youth provider where applicable
- emergency reporting and incident flagging built into the flow

### 5. Geography & Time Limits
- local radius limits
- no late-night bookings for youth accounts
- category-specific availability windows
- optional parent approval required per booking

## Suggested Operational Flow

### A. Parent / Guardian Onboarding
1. Adult creates a Go-Gig account
2. Adult selects “Add Youth Provider”
3. Adult verifies:
   - full legal name
   - relationship to youth
   - youth date of birth
   - emergency contact info
4. Adult agrees to Youth Provider terms and parental consent

### B. Youth Profile Setup
1. Add youth first name and last initial only, if you want extra privacy
2. Upload approved profile photo
3. Select approved service categories
4. Set service radius
5. Add short bio / skills / portfolio
6. Mark whether parent approval is required for every booking

### C. Booking Flow
1. Customer sees youth badge and trust disclosure
2. Customer requests service
3. Parent/guardian receives request notice
4. Parent/guardian approves or declines
5. Booking confirmed
6. Work completed
7. Customer rates provider
8. Payout sent to parent/guardian wallet or bank account

### D. Incident Flow
1. Customer or parent can report a safety issue
2. Booking immediately paused if issue is severe
3. Youth account can be frozen pending review
4. All incident actions logged with timestamps

### 1. Parties
This agreement is entered into by the undersigned parent or legal guardian (“Parent/Guardian”) in connection with the participation of the minor identified below (“Youth Provider”) on the Go-Gig platform.

### 2. Platform-Only Role
Parent/Guardian acknowledges that Go-Gig is a technology platform that enables customers and independent providers to discover one another and arrange services. Go-Gig is **not**:
- the employer of the Youth Provider
- a staffing agency
- a school program operator
- a supervisor of the Youth Provider’s work
- a transportation provider
- a guarantor of customer conduct or jobsite safety

### 3. Parent/Guardian Authority
Parent/Guardian represents and warrants that:
- they are the lawful parent or legal guardian of the Youth Provider
- they have authority to consent to the Youth Provider’s participation
- all information provided to Go-Gig is accurate
- they will review bookings and supervise the Youth Provider as appropriate

### 4. Parent-Controlled Participation
Parent/Guardian understands and agrees that:
- the Youth Provider’s participation is subject to Go-Gig policy restrictions
- Go-Gig may limit job categories, geography, hours, or messaging functions
- all payouts for under-18 Youth Providers will be issued to the Parent/Guardian or an adult-controlled payout account
- Parent/Guardian is responsible for any onward transfer of funds to the Youth Provider

### 5. Acknowledgment of Risk
Parent/Guardian understands that offering local services involves risk, including but not limited to:
- interaction with customers and third parties
- travel to and from service locations
- potential bodily injury
- property damage
- disputes about service quality or conduct

Parent/Guardian voluntarily accepts these risks on behalf of themselves and the Youth Provider, to the maximum extent allowed by law.

### 6. Compliance Responsibility
Parent/Guardian agrees that they, and not Go-Gig, are responsible for determining whether the Youth Provider may legally perform the selected services under applicable federal, state, and local law, including any youth work rules, permit rules, school rules, or curfew-related restrictions.

### 7. Restricted Services
Parent/Guardian agrees that the Youth Provider will only perform services permitted by Go-Gig policy and approved on the account. Parent/Guardian agrees not to use Go-Gig for hazardous, illegal, age-restricted, or otherwise prohibited work.

### 8. Release of Claims
To the fullest extent permitted by law, Parent/Guardian releases Go-Gig and its officers, directors, employees, contractors, and affiliates from claims arising out of or related to:
- the Youth Provider’s participation on the platform
- customer interactions
- service performance
- injuries, losses, or disputes occurring during or around booked services

This release does not apply where prohibited by law.

### 9. Indemnification
Parent/Guardian agrees to defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Go-Gig from third-party claims, losses, liabilities, damages, or expenses arising from:
- the Youth Provider’s acts or omissions
- the Parent/Guardian’s breach of these terms
- violations of law or policy relating to the Youth Provider’s participation

to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law.

### 10. No Insurance Provided
Parent/Guardian understands that Go-Gig does not provide insurance for the Youth Provider at this stage, including workers’ compensation, medical coverage, general liability, or accident coverage.

### 11. Right to Suspend or Remove
Go-Gig may suspend, limit, or remove any Youth Provider account or related adult account at any time if it believes there is safety, trust, legal, or policy risk.

## Recommended Product Copy

Customer-facing disclosure:
- “Youth Provider, parent-approved account”
- “Bookings for this provider are managed through a parent/guardian account”

Internal positioning:
- “Youth Provider Program”
- “Student Talent Program”

Avoid:
- “kids working”
- “teen labor marketplace”
- anything that sounds exploitative or unsupervised

---

## Risk Reduction Checklist

Before launch:
- lawyer review of terms, consent, and classification posture
- review state-specific rules for 16-17 year olds in target launch states
- clear prohibited-services matrix
- reporting/escalation workflow
- parent-controlled payout setup
- minimum documentation retention for consent logs
- moderation policy for customer behavior and incident handling

Strongly recommended later:
- marketplace liability insurance
- accident/incident procedure
- optional background review for customers in certain categories
- abuse prevention rules for messages and off-platform contact

---

## Suggested MVP Launch Strategy

Phase 1:
- ages 16-17 only
- invite or pilot-based rollout
- limited categories
- parent-controlled payouts
- one city or one school/community cohort

Phase 2:
- expand categories carefully
- introduce provider badges and student portfolios
- collect safety and trust data before scaling

Phase 3:
- only consider younger minors if counsel says the structure is clean enough

## Founder Notes

This idea could help Go-Gig grow because it taps into:
- young people who want income
- parents who want structure and safety
- communities that like supporting student talent
- schools/churches/youth groups that want real-world opportunity pathways

But the winning version is **safe, parent-controlled, and clearly marketplace-only**.
If it feels loose, exploitative, or confusing, it will backfire fast.
